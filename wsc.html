Welcome world scholars of Kabarak.Let's start with an intro to the competition that brought you here, The World Scholar's Cup.The cup is a competition that brings scholars from all around the world to rounds to compete in 4 different events:

Team Debate
Three debates against other teams with 'unique' motions like "All people should wear shoes that make them the same height" and "World leaders should be chosen by an Artificial Intelligence."
Collaborative Writing
One topic from each subject and 1hr to write 3 essays with your teammates, You can write a play, a story, a dialogue or whatever you want. It's a rush against time and we usually waste time drawing an alpaca on our scripts.
The Scholar's Challenge
The Scholar's Challange is WSC staff's name for the 1hr of hell scholars go through every round. 120 multiple choice questions, 20 from every subject and absolutely no chance of answering every question unless you're guessing.
The Scholars' Bowl
Probably the most fun event of the cup. One clicker per team and several questions awarded marks according to the mood of WSC staff. It's a great bonding experience for your team as you argue over which answer to choose.

There's three rounds: The Regional Round, The Global Round and The Tournament of Champions. To prepare for each round you should be well-versed with the WSC syllabus which can be found on WSC's official site. On a team of three, each member should specialize on two subjects and have a rudimentary grasp of the other four. The subject topics change per year but as of 2022 we're still using the 2020 syllabus due to the stall in WSC events caused by COVID-19.the topics are:
